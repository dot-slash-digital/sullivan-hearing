Hearing Loss and Your Health

Hearing Loss & Brain Health

Studies show that untreated hearing loss increases the risk of cognitive decline. While hearing aids don't prevent dementia, they help you stay connected with people, which keeps your brain active and healthy.

Keep Your Brain Active

Hearing stimulates your brain. When hearing decreases, brain stimulation decreases too. This can affect memory, attention span and decision-making. Better hearing means a more active brain.

Reduce Mental Fatigue

Straining to hear conversations forces your brain to work overtime. This leaves less energy for remembering what was said. Hearing aids reduce this mental workload.

Stay Connected

Hearing loss often leads to social withdrawal. Many studies link isolation to health problems. Hearing aids help you stay engaged with family, friends and colleagues.
